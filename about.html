A Letter From The Founder

Real Tokyo,
beyond the
guidebooks.

Until about a year ago, I was a backpacker. The kind of traveller who measures a city not by its monuments but by how easily a stranger will pull up a chair and ask where you're from.

I'd been on the road long enough to forget what coming home felt like. Then I came back to Japan — and remembered.

It wasn't the skyline or the trains or the convenience-store coffee, though those are all genuinely good. It was the people. The shop owner who walks you halfway down the street to make sure you find the right alley. The chef who learns your name on a Tuesday and remembers it the following Friday. The quiet, unfussy warmth of a country that doesn't perform for you — it just lets you in, slowly, if you stay long enough to notice.

I rediscovered how warm and welcoming people can be here — and it inspired me to share that feeling through my tours. — Founder, Michitabi

That's the part you can't print in a guidebook. Guidebooks send you to Shibuya Crossing and Senso-ji. They're great. But they don't send you to the standing bar where the owner pours you a beer and asks what you do for work, or the family-run sushi counter that's been open for three generations and seats six people.

So I started taking people there myself. Through local, unique experiences, I want guests to go beyond sightseeing and genuinely connect with the warmth of everyday Japan. That's it. That's the whole idea.

道 · The Path

What started as me showing one or two travellers around turned into something bigger than I planned. My friends got involved — chefs, photographers, fellow wanderers — each with their own corner of the city to share. We don't run scripts. We don't run schedules. We figure out who you are over the first ten minutes, and then we build the day around that.

Some days that means hidden shrines and matcha and the good kind of long walks. Some days it means street food and thrift shops in Shimokitazawa until your feet hurt. Sometimes it means standing on a bridge in Meguro at the exact moment the cherry blossoms decide to fall, even though it's raining and we'd planned to be somewhere else entirely.

However it goes — that's Michitabi. The road, and the journey, and the people you meet along the way. We'd love for you to be one of them.
